The water cycle, also known as the hydrological cycle, describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the Earth's surface. It involves several key processes: evaporation, where water from oceans, rivers, and lakes turns into vapor; condensation, where the vapor cools and forms clouds; precipitation, where water falls as rain, snow, or hail; and collection, where water returns to bodies of water or seeps into the ground. Additionally, transpiration from plants releases moisture into the air. This natural cycle is essential for distributing fresh water, supporting ecosystems, and maintaining the Earth's climate balance.Description
