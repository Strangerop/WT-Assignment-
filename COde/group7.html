Wind energy is generated using windmills or wind turbines, which convert the kinetic energy of wind into electricity. When the wind blows, it moves the turbine blades, which are connected to a rotor. The rotor spins a shaft linked to a generator, producing electrical energy. This clean and renewable energy source reduces reliance on fossil fuels and helps lower carbon emissions. Wind farms, consisting of multiple wind turbines, are commonly set up in areas with strong, consistent winds, such as coastal regions or open plains. Advances in wind technology continue to improve efficiency, making wind power a key player in sustainable energy.Description
